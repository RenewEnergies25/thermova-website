🔋 Why add a battery?

Solar panels generate during the day. Without a battery, surplus electricity is exported to the grid at around 5p/kWh. With a battery, you store it and use it yourself at 25–30p/kWh worth of savings — five to six times more valuable.

A battery also lets you charge from the grid overnight on cheap off-peak tariffs and discharge during expensive peak hours — saving money even on grey days.

Common questions

Heat pump & grant questions, answered simply

The questions most homeowners and landlords ask before booking a home assessment.

Does my property qualify for the £7,500 grant?
The Boiler Upgrade Scheme is available to homeowners and landlords with properties in England and Wales. The property must have a valid EPC, and the upgrade must replace a fossil fuel heating system (gas, oil, LPG or electric). Most new builds and social housing are excluded. One grant per property. We check eligibility as part of your free home assessment — you don't need to work this out yourself.
Do I have to apply for the grant myself?
No. As an approved BUS installer, Thermova applies for the grant on your behalf through the Ofgem portal. The £7,500 is deducted from your installation invoice — you don't wait for a rebate or fill in any government forms. It works like an instant discount at the point of install.
Do heat pumps work in older UK homes?
Yes — modern air source heat pumps are designed to work effectively in a wide range of UK homes, including older properties. The system is designed around your home's specific heat loss, and we factor in radiator sizing, insulation and flow temperatures at the survey stage. Heat pumps are not only for new builds or heavily insulated homes.
Are landlords eligible for the BUS grant?
Yes. The Boiler Upgrade Scheme is available to owners of domestic properties, including those rented out privately. The grant is for the property, not the owner's income or circumstances. For landlords planning ahead for the 2030 rental standard, BUS funding also does not count toward the £10,000 per-property cost cap set out in the government's updated PRS guidance.
As a landlord, am I required to install a heat pump under the 2030 rules?
No — the government has been clear that it does not intend to force landlords to install heat pumps under the updated private rental sector regulations. The route to compliance is flexible: fabric-first improvements, a smart-readiness route, or a heating system route. We help landlords plan the most practical and cost-effective upgrade path for each property.
Does solar work in the UK, even in winter?
Yes. Solar panels generate electricity from daylight, not direct sunshine — so they produce power year-round, including on overcast days. Output is naturally lower in winter, but a properly designed system combined with a battery means you can store spring and autumn generation for use year-round. With smart tariffs, you can also charge the battery overnight at off-peak rates (as low as 6–7p/kWh) and use that stored electricity during the day instead of paying 25–30p/kWh from the grid. We size solar systems for your actual usage, not best-case summer conditions.
What are the typical savings from solar panels and a battery?
For a typical 3-bedroom home, solar panels alone can save around £400–£800 per year. Adding a battery can increase that to £800–£1,400 per year, depending on your household's energy usage, roof orientation and tariff. The battery is more valuable than it looks on paper: solar electricity you use yourself is worth around 25–30p/kWh in avoided grid costs, while electricity exported without a battery earns only around 5p/kWh. Savings figures are estimates — we'll model your specific home at the assessment stage.
How long does a heat pump installation take?
Most air source heat pump installations take one to three days. We'll give you a precise estimate after the property survey. We aim to minimise disruption — in most cases heating is only interrupted during the day of the final switchover, and you'll have heat again by the evening.
Can I combine a heat pump with solar and battery?
Absolutely — and it's often the most cost-effective approach. A heat pump runs on electricity, so generating your own via solar reduces the running cost of the pump. Add a battery and you can use stored solar electricity to power the heat pump even when the sun isn't shining. We design whole-home energy systems that combine all three for maximum effect.
