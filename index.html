Heat pump vs gas boiler

Why people switch even when electricity costs more

The unit price objection is real, so the page should answer it directly. What matters is system efficiency, lifespan, future energy direction, and what happens once solar or smart tariffs are part of the picture.

Gas boiler

Like-for-like default
  • EfficiencyUp to 90%
  • Fuel costAbout 7p/kWh gas
  • Running costAbout £800-£900 per year
  • Lifespan10-15 years
  • CarbonHigh and tied to fossil fuel
  • Long-term fitWeak as policy and infrastructure move away from gas

Air source heat pump

Thermova route
  • Efficiency250-400% with proper design
  • Fuel costAbout 25p/kWh electricity
  • Running costOften comparable, and cheaper with solar or smart tariffs
  • Lifespan15-25 years
  • CarbonLower now and improving as the grid greens
  • Long-term fitBetter aligned with where home energy is heading
Running costs for heat pumps and gas boilers are broadly comparable at current tariffs for a typical 3-bed home, but the economics improve materially when you add solar panels, use a heat-pump tariff, or replace an older inefficient boiler. The bigger advantage is long-term: lower carbon, longer system life, and less dependency on gas.

Solar panels and battery storage

Your roof could power your heat pump and more of your home

A heat pump runs on electricity. Pair it with solar panels and a battery and more of that electricity comes from your own roof instead of the grid.

Solar alone

Typical annual savings of about £400-£800 depending on home size, roof orientation, and daytime use.

Solar plus battery

Typical annual savings of about £800-£1,400 when more generated power is stored and used in the home.

Why a battery changes the logic

Without a battery, spare solar often leaves the home at low export value. With a battery, more of that power gets used at avoided-grid value instead. It also lets you buy cheap overnight electricity and use it during expensive periods.

  • Solar powers the heat pump during the day for near-zero marginal cost.
  • The battery stores surplus generation for evening use.
  • Cheap off-peak charging improves the economics on grey days.
  • EV charging can sit inside the same system logic.

Best-fit homes for solar

  • Homes with strong daytime electricity use
  • EV owners and work-from-home households
  • Properties planning a heat pump upgrade
  • Roofs with good orientation and limited shading

Waiting has a cost here too

Every year you delay, you buy more expensive grid electricity and miss the chance to offset more of your own heating and household load.

Before enquiry

Does my property qualify for the £7,500 grant?
The Boiler Upgrade Scheme is available to homeowners and landlords with properties in England and Wales. The property must have a valid EPC, and the upgrade must replace a fossil fuel heating system. Most new builds and social housing are excluded. One grant per property. We check eligibility as part of your free home assessment.
Do I have to apply for the grant myself?
No. As an approved BUS installer, Thermova applies on your behalf through the Ofgem portal. The £7,500 is deducted from your installation invoice.

Heat pump concerns

Do heat pumps work in older UK homes?
Yes. Modern air source heat pumps can work effectively in a wide range of UK homes, including older properties. The key is proper design around heat loss, radiator sizing, insulation, and flow temperatures.
How long does a heat pump installation take?
Most air source heat pump installations take one to three days. We give a specific estimate after the property survey and aim to minimise disruption during the final switchover.
Can I combine a heat pump with solar and battery?
Yes, and it is often the strongest whole-home route. Solar reduces the running cost of the pump and the battery helps carry more of that value into the evening.

Solar concerns

Does solar work in the UK, even in winter?
Yes. Solar panels generate electricity from daylight, not direct sunshine. Output is lower in winter than summer, but systems still generate year-round and batteries or smart tariffs improve how much value you keep.
What are the typical savings from solar panels and a battery?
For a typical 3-bedroom home, solar alone can save around £400-£800 per year. Adding a battery can increase that to roughly £800-£1,400 depending on usage, roof orientation, and tariff.

Landlords

Are landlords eligible for the BUS grant?
Yes. The Boiler Upgrade Scheme is available to owners of domestic properties, including private landlords. The grant applies to the property, not the owner's income or circumstances.
Am I required to install a heat pump under the 2030 rules?
No. The route to compliance is not limited to one technology. We help landlords understand the most practical and cost-effective path for each property rather than forcing one template onto the whole portfolio.
